What is a Rubik's cube

A Rubik's Cube is a three-dimensional puzzle toy consisting of 27 smaller cubes that can be rotated to align colors on each face, challenging solvers to develop spatial reasoning and problem-solving skills.

It was invented in 1974 by Ernő Rubik and has since become one of the world's most popular puzzles.

The cube has six faces, each covered by nine stickers of a single color, and the goal is to twist and turn the cube to return it to its original state, where each face is a solid color.

Solving the cube requires understanding its mechanics, learning algorithms, and practicing to recognize patterns, making it a rewarding and mentally stimulating activity for people of all ages.
